ICE CREAM

Properties of Ice cream

Texture:

Ice cream should have a smooth, creamy texture, which is achieved through proper churning and the
incorporation of air (overrun).

Temperature:

Typically served at around -10 to -20oC (14 to -4oF), ice cream needs to remain cold to maintain its
structure and avoidmelting.

Flavor:

Ice cream can have a wide variety of flavors, influenced by the base ingredients (like cream, milk, and
sugar) and additional flavorings (fruits, chocolate, nuts, etc.).

Sweetness:

The sugar content not only sweetens but also affects the Freezing point, helping to create a softer texture.

Fat Content:

The fat in ice cream (from cream or milk) contributes to creaminess and richness. Higher fat content
generally results in a smoother texture.

Stability:

Ingredients like stabilizers (e.g., guar gum, carrageenan) help prevent ice crystal formation, ensuring a consistent texture.

Mouthfeel:

The sensation of ice cream in the mouth is influenced by its fat content, temperature, and air
incorporation, all contributing to its creaminess.

Melting Rate:

Ice cream's melting rate can vary based on its ingredients, particularly fat and air content, affecting how
it feels when consumed.

Serving and Storage:

storage is crucial; Ice cream should be kept at a consistent temperature to prevent freezer burn and maintain quality.
